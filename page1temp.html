I've learned some things over the years.

It all started back in 2015 with the release of Undertale, my favorite video game ever. I felt awakened by the great story, the abstract gameplay, and the inspiring tale of one guy making a whole video game - including all of the music - with the help of his artist friends.  I had to find people to talk to about it - and that led me to Discord. Little did I know that Discord was my gateway to many lasting friendships and real programming skills. From it I've learned LUA, HTML, CSS, Javascript, and C#.

The battle I put the most work into - a fight against Chara, the enigmatic evil child in Undertale's story.

UNITALE

The first programming language I learned was LUA, the main language of UNITALE, a Unity-based engine for making Undertale-themed battles. There I learned how to program waves, make objects wobble, rotate, and stab smoothly, and make loose attempts at storytelling.

Trick or Treat

This was my first gamejam project, created in a single weekend on the Unreal engine with two other people. Trick or Treat is your standard spooky game where a monster pursues you through darkened woods, as you collect pumpkins on the ground.

Cave Hero

Cave Hero is the first project I've participated in specifically for a game design class. Follow a stranded adventurer as they mine rocks, buy upgrades and discover a shocking secret about the king of the slime people! This project is of considerably higher polish than the others, and I had a great time programming the motions for the enemies, fire effects, and attack animations.

You can check out Cave Hero here!

Zoomstick

Zoomstick is the second video game I've made with a team. In Zoomstick, you play as temple robber who finds an ancient shotgun capable of propelling the user high into the air. In this game, I wrote interpretive code used in loading/unloading levels.

You can check out Zoomstick here!
