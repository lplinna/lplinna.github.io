Welcome!

I'm Luke Linna - an aspiring programmer with a particular set of skills. Here you can learn who I am and what I do.

Who am i?

Well, it's easiest to answer that question with where I live first. I come from Green Bay, Wisconsin, where my parents live in the middle of a large and somewhat boring suburb. With anything notable being a thirty-minute walk in any direction, it's easy to see why I preferred staying inside and gaming over having adventures in the open.

My dad is a programmer for the local school district, and he's part of the reason that I got into gaming. I love playing video games with him or sharing the latest and greatest game to get me excited.

My mom has both impaired vision and hearing, to the level that she needs a cane to navigate outside and hearing aids to listen to conversations. Through her, I learned that no matter what problems you may have you can still live a powerful and fulfilling life.

In high school, I met friends who had very different existences than mine. I learned quickly that my life was one of comfort and privilege; I got along with everyone in my family, we rarely had financial concerns, and safety was everything but guaranteed. For many, that's not always the case.

You know the drill: at my age, I'll take any job I can get. But I'd much rather make something inspiring, something that might help a bad day become a little better for someone out there. And quite often, the most effective way to do this is a nice, cathartic video game that pulls you in, develops your emotions, and spits you out into the real world again.
Currently I'm attending University of Wisconsin - Stout for a B.S in Computer Science with an emphasis in game design. I aspire to be a part of the game design industry, producing emotional and fun experiences that people will love.
