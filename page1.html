I've learned some things over the years.

It all started back in 2015 with the release of Undertale, my favorite video game ever. I felt awakened by the great story, the abstract gameplay, and the inspiring tale of one guy making a whole video game - including all of the music - with the help of his artist friends. 

I had to find people to talk to about it - and that led me to Discord. Little did I know that Discord was my gateway to many lasting friendships and real programming skills. Thanks to Discord, I've learned LUA, HTML, CSS, Javascript, and C#.

The battle I put the most work into - a fight against Chara, the enigmatic evil child in Undertale's story.

UNITALE

The first programming language I learned was LUA, the main language of UNITALE, a Unity-based engine for making Undertale-themed battles.

There I learned how to program waves, make objects wobble, rotate, and stab smoothly, and make loose attempts at storytelling.

That classic Unreal lighting.

Trick or Treat

This was my first gamejam project, created in a single weekend on the Unreal engine with two other people.
Trick or Treat is your standard spooky game where a monster pursues you through darkened woods, as you collect pumpkins on the ground.

You can check it out here!

Cave Hero

Cave Hero is the first project I've participated in specifically for a game design class. Follow a stranded adventurer as they mine rocks, buy upgrades and discover a shocking secret about the king of the slime people!

This project is of considerably higher polish than the others, and I had a great time programming the motions for the enemies, fire effects, and attack animations.

You can check out Cave Hero here!
